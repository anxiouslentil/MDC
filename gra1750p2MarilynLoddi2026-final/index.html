You can recreate this exact sculpture, or gather these materials and let your own enteric wisdom take over, building something entirely yours. Either way, you’re feeding the microbes within and the creative impulse that lives in your gut. For inspiration watch this video.

Ingredients

  • Tofu
  • Dried rosemary
  • Peppercorns
  • Tofu
  • Sprouted pumpkin seeds
  • 1 Strawberry
  • 1 Baby carrot
  • 1tbs Turmeric
  • 1tbs Paprika
  • 1tsp Golden flax poweder
  • 1 Boiled egg
  • Beet sauerkraut juice
  • ¼ Cup of grapes (green/red)
  • ¼ Cup Blueberries
  • Sliced almonds

Instructions

  1. Slice tofu into a rectangular base and press fresh pumpkin seeds into the front side to create a studded pattern. Press dried rosemary into the left and right side, placing peppercorns inbetween the rosemary.
  2. Slice a medium round from a strawberry and place on top of the tofu.
  3. Cut a baby carrot into four thick rounds and layer on top of the strawberry.
  4. Slice tofu into a smaller thick rectangle, dust it with turmeric and paprika.
  5. Dust the center of the strawberry with golden flax powder.
  6. Brush honey onto the carrots and place the dusted tofu on top, pressing it down gently.
  7. Peel a hard-boiled egg and slice it in half lengthwise. Place one half cut-side out on top of the tofu.
  8. Insert two slices of almonds at angles on either side like reaching arms.
  9. Slice grapes vertivally and blueberries horizontally and arrange them decoratively behind the base tofu in whatever pattern you desire.
  10. Pour beet sauerkraut juice over the boiled egg letting it pour down to the tofu base.
  11. Step back and adjust elements by intuition until the sculpture feels complete.
